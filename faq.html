FAQ's

What is Prevent Safeguarding?

Prevent Safeguarding is a part of the UK's broader counter-terrorism strategy known as CONTEST. The aim is to prevent people from being drawn into terrorism and to ensure that individuals who are vulnerable to radicalization are given appropriate support.

Who is responsible for Prevent Safeguarding?

Prevent Safeguarding is a multi-agency approach involving schools, colleges, universities, health services, local authorities, the police, and community organizations.

What are the key objectives of Prevent?

The key objectives are to challenge the ideology that supports terrorism, protect vulnerable individuals, and support sectors and institutions where there is a risk of radicalization.

Icon/image How are individuals identified as vulnerable to radicalization?

Indicators may include changes in behavior, expressing extremist views, and association with known extremists. Referrals can be made by teachers, social workers, healthcare professionals, and concerned community members.

What happens after a referral is made?

Referrals are reviewed by a multi-agency panel called Channel, which assesses the risk and decides on the most appropriate action, which may include providing support through mentoring, counseling, or other interventions.

What is the Channel Program?

The Channel Program is a key element of Prevent, which focuses on providing support to individuals who are at risk of being drawn into terrorism. It is a confidential and voluntary process.

What kind of support is provided to individuals?

Support can include educational support, vocational training, mental health services, and support from faith leaders or community groups to address specific vulnerabilities.

Is Prevent Safeguarding mandatory?

Yes, under the Counter-Terrorism and Security Act 2015, specified authorities such as schools, universities, and healthcare providers have a statutory duty to have due regard to the need to prevent people from being drawn into terrorism.

How does Prevent respect human rights and individual freedoms?

Prevent aims to balance safeguarding individuals from radicalization while respecting individual rights and freedoms. Measures taken are intended to be proportionate, and there are safeguards in place to protect against discrimination.

How can concerns about radicalization be reported?

Concerns can be reported to local authorities, the police, or directly to Prevent leads within specific organizations. There are also helplines and online reporting tools available.

What training is available for professionals?

Prevent training is available for professionals in various sectors, helping them to understand the risks of radicalization, recognize warning signs, and know how to refer individuals for support.

Icon/image What are some criticisms of the Prevent strategy?

Criticisms include concerns about stigmatization of certain communities, impacts on free speech, and effectiveness of the strategy. The government and other bodies regularly review and update Prevent to address these concerns. Understanding Prevent Safeguarding and its implementation is essential for ensuring that individuals and communities are protected from the threats of radicalization and terrorism while maintaining a balance with human rights and freedoms. link
